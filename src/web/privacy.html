Privacy Policy

Effective date: May 7, 2026  ·  Last updated: May 7, 2026

Gravio ("we", "our", "us") operates the website at gravio.dev and the Gravio CLI. This policy explains what data we collect, how we use it, and what choices you have. By using Gravio you agree to this policy.

1. Information we collect

Account data. When you register, we store your email address and a hashed password (or OAuth token if you sign in with Google). We never store plain-text passwords.

Project metadata. We store project names, scan timestamps, aggregate quality scores, and trend data that you submit through the CLI or dashboard.

Scan payloads. Run payloads (the detailed JSON your CLI submits) are end-to-end encrypted before they leave your machine. The server stores ciphertext only. We cannot read the contents of your scan data.

Usage logs. We log HTTP request metadata (method, path, status code, duration) for operational monitoring. Logs do not contain request or response bodies.

Cookies & session tokens. We use a single session cookie to keep you signed in. No advertising or tracking cookies are set.

2. How we use your information

  • To authenticate you and provide access to your projects and dashboard.
  • To store and display your quality scores and trends.
  • To send transactional emails (password reset, account notices) — no marketing emails without your consent.
  • To monitor service health, diagnose errors, and improve reliability.

We do not sell, rent, or share your personal data with third parties for marketing purposes.

3. End-to-end encryption

Gravio uses client-side encryption for all run payloads. Your data is encrypted with a key derived from your account before it reaches our servers. Only you — or someone you share the decryption key with — can read the contents of your scan runs. We have no ability to decrypt your run data even if compelled to do so.

4. Data retention

Account data and project metadata are retained for as long as your account is active. Encrypted run payloads are retained until you delete the project or your account. You may request full account deletion at any time by emailing hello@gravio.dev — we will purge all your data within 30 days.

5. Third-party services

We use the following third-party services to operate Gravio:

  • Fly.io — infrastructure and hosting. Data is processed in their data centres. See Fly.io Privacy Policy.
  • Google OAuth — optional sign-in provider. If you use "Sign in with Google", Google processes your OAuth token according to the Google Privacy Policy. We only receive your email address and a stable identifier.

6. Your rights

Depending on your jurisdiction, you may have the right to access, correct, or delete personal data we hold about you; to restrict or object to processing; and to data portability. To exercise any of these rights, contact us at hello@gravio.dev.

7. Security

We use HTTPS for all communication, store passwords with bcrypt, and enforce session expiry. Despite these measures, no system is perfectly secure. Please use a strong, unique password and notify us immediately if you suspect unauthorised access to your account.

8. Children

Gravio is not directed at children under 16. We do not knowingly collect personal data from anyone under 16. If you believe a child has provided us with their data, please contact us and we will delete it promptly.

9. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time. We will post the revised policy on this page with an updated effective date. Continued use of Gravio after the effective date constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.

Questions? Email us at hello@gravio.dev. We aim to respond within 5 business days.
